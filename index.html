Research

Difference sets have been studied for decades due to their applications in digital communication, cryptography, algebra, and number theory. Almost difference sets, on the other hand, have similar functionalities, yet with more potential of finding new constructions. We explored almost difference sets in cyclic and non-cyclic groups to search for possible constructions, during which I also build programming tools to automate and speed up the exploration process. To learn more about what I did, feel free to check out my Honor's thesis here!

Reconfiguration problems have been studied and applied to solve problems in various areas, including in Math, Computer science, and Chemistry. Due to the close relatedness between coloring graphs and reconfiguration problem, the connectivity and structure of coloring graphs give valuable information to a solution set of the corresponding reconfiguration problems. We've been exploring 2-connectedness and cut-vertices of coloring graphs, and forbidden structures on a coloring graph with cut-vertices. We have also built a computing and visualization tool to assist our research process.
